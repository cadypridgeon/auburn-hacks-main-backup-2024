Auburn Hacks

February 3-4, 2024

About

Auburn Hacks is a 24-hour event where students from universities across the Southeast, including Auburn University, unite to build technical projects. Our hackathon offers inclusive workshops to equip students with the tools needed for outstanding project development. Participants showcase their creations for judging, competing for exciting prizes.

We foster a supportive atmosphere, welcoming individuals of all skill levels and backgrounds. Come engage in networking sessions with sponsoring companies to create connections that shape your future. Join us on February 3-4, 2024, for Alabama's largest hackathon!

Hack with Us!

FAQ

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is a collaborative event spanning one or two days where participants collaborate to create a project of their choice. Though they can be coding-focused, no prior programming experience is necessary!

When and where is the hackathon?

Auburn Hacks starts Saturday, February 3, 2024, and ends Sunday, February 4, 2024. It is located in the Brown-Kopel Engineering Student Achievement Center on Auburn University's campus.

Who can participate?

Current college students or graduates within the past year are eligible. Undergraduates, graduates, and students from any university are welcome at Auburn Hacks!

Do I need coding experience to join?

No coding experience? No problem! Auburn Hacks is a learning platform for all tech enthusiasts. Join us to learn and explore!

How do I register for the hackathon?

Secure your spot by registering here! Registration closes on January 31, 2024, at 11:59 PM CST. For inquiries, email us!

Is there a registration fee?

Participation at Auburn Hacks is free for everyone!

What should I bring to the hackathon?

Pack essentials like your laptop, chargers, and any tech tools. For the 24-hour event, consider a sleeping bag, toiletries, and lots of enthusiasm!

What should I wear for the hackathon?

Comfort is key! Plan for 24 hours and bring a change of clothes. We have some exciting optional activities planned!

Will there be mentors or workshops available?

Yes, we will have workshops going on throughout the event. If a topic interests you, please stop by! Additionally, we will have a couple of mentors present during the event who can assist with technical questions.

What are the prizes or awards for winners?

Yes, there will be cash prizes for the top three projects! There may also be sponsor prizes available.

Is there a theme or specific problem to solve?

Our theme this year is Cosmic Escape! While we do not require participants to create a themed project, it is encouraged. Projects of all backgrounds are welcomed!

What is the event schedule?

As the hackathon nears, a live site will be published with the full hackathon schedule and other resources. If you register, you will receive an email with the live site and the full schedule attached.

What kind of projects are accepted?

All technical projects are accepted at Auburn Hacks! We encourage projects that showcase innovative software and/or hardware development. Whether it's a web app, mobile application, IoT device, or any creative tech solution, all ideas are welcome.

How are teams formed?

If you do not have a team before arriving at the hackathon, we will have a social event during the first hour of check-ins for hackers to meet each other. There, you will be able to form a team!

Can I participate if I don't have a team?

Yes, of course! In our social hour before the Opening Ceremony, you will have the chance to meet other hackers, as well as our sponsors!

What resources or tools will be provided?

Our Auburn Hacks Notion will be available to participants during the event. There, you will find tutorials, online resources, and project prompts. Additionally, we will have a couple of mentors available for questions during the event.

Will food and accommodations be provided?

Participants will receive free lunch, dinner, breakfast, and snacks. Unfortunately, Auburn Hacks will not provide compensation to those traveling to Auburn. Please email us! here if you have any questions.

How will judging or presentations work?

At the end of the hackathon, teams will exhibit their projects for evaluation by a diverse panel of industry professionals and academics.

Can I start working on my project before the event?

No, all project development must occur during Auburn Hacks.

Is there a code of conduct for the event?

Everyone who participates must abide by the MLH Code of Conduct. Our biggest rule is that all work must be done at Auburn Hacks.

What COVID-19 safety measures are in place?

Auburn Hacks adheres to university policies. Currently, Auburn Hacks is in-person, with optional masks. If exposed to COVID-19, please follow CDC guidelines before participating.

How can I contact the organizers for further questions?

If you have any other questions, please email us! here!
